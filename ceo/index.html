The missing layer

LLMs are the interface.
The model is the evidence.

Most "AI GTM" today is LLM-heavy and science-light. LLMs summarise notes, generate outreach, and draft frameworks. But they don't train directly on your revenue data, they don't produce quantified confidence, and they can't give you an audit trail a board can trust.

What's missing is the scientific foundation layer:

Predictive models on your data

Historical sales and engagement data turned into win propensity, ICP fit, churn risk, and deal health models. Trained, tested, and validated.

Quantified confidence

"This deal has an 82% probability of closing this quarter." Not a guess. A model output with clear features, assumptions, and limitations.

Board-ready audit trail

Clear documentation of what drives each prediction. When the board asks "how do you know?", you have the answer.

Then, and only then, should LLMs sit on top as the interface and accelerator, not the foundation.
